Welcome to CCBI

CCBI is a local NGO commited to empowering disadvantaged communities and promoting sustainable livelihoods through capacity building, economic empowerment, and behavior change communication.

Genesis

CCBI began by observing pregnant unwed teen girls dropping out of school near Mwurile cell. We identified root causes, implemented preventive measures, and supported victims to ensure their children's welfare

Vision

To create a positive, lasting change within communities through comprehensive and impactful initiatives

Mission

To empower disadvantaged populations, by breaking the cycle of poverty, enhancing their well-being, and unlocking their full potential

Our Focus

Capacity building

At CCBI through comprehensive capacity-building programs, we empower youth and teen mothers. Our programs are centered on offering opportunities for entrepreneurship, mentorship, life skills development, and basic education.

Economic empowerment

At CCBI, we empower teenage mothers through comprehensive support programs and skill development. Our approach includes counseling sessions to address unique challenges, an integrated curriculum to equip mothers with essential skills, and post-graduation collaboration.

Behavioral change communication(BCC)

Our BCC program is designed to promote positive attitudes, increase self-esteem, and instill agency in these teen mothers. Through counseling groups and individual counseling sessions, we address each mother's unique challenges and promote positive behavior change that supports personal and professional growth.

Our Objective

Women empowerment

Group and one-on-one counseling sessions are held, focusing on each young mother's unique issues. We focus on initiatives promoting positive attitudes and how they can work hard to earn money to support themselves and their children.

Community meetings

Parents of the teen mothers, para-socials, friends of the families, and local leaders are engaged in discussions concerning family welfare. Raising children in non-abusive environments, fostering harmony within the family

Youth engagements

12-18 years actively participate in solving issues that affect them, including poverty, early pregnancy, and drug use and abuse. These courses are designed with young people in mind. They have also started savings programs with the help of CCBI
